January 2025

Why I Started Mountain West Surface

Starting a business was never really in my long-term plan. I'd always been drawn to technology, coding, and automation. But sometimes the best opportunities come from unexpected places.

The Spark

After working in various service roles, I noticed a gap in the commercial cleaning market. Many businesses struggled to find reliable, quality-focused cleaning services. The idea for Mountain West Surface grew from a simple question: what if we could do this better?

Building from the Ground Up

The early days were challenging. From learning the ins and outs of commercial cleaning to building client relationships, every day brought new lessons. But my background in tech helped me approach problems systematically.

  • Systems Thinking: I applied the same logic I use in coding to business operations
  • Automation: Even a cleaning company can benefit from automated scheduling and communication
  • Quality Focus: Just like clean code, clean spaces require attention to detail

Looking Forward

Mountain West Surface continues to grow, and I'm excited about the future. The blend of hands-on business experience and tech innovation creates unique opportunities to serve our clients better every day.

December 2024

Lessons from Building Trading Bots

Algorithmic trading has been one of my most educational projects. Not because of profits (that's a whole other story), but because of what it teaches about systems, risk, and patience.

The Allure of Automation

The idea of a program that makes money while you sleep is incredibly appealing. Who wouldn't want that? But the reality is far more complex than any YouTube video suggests.

Key Lessons Learned

  • Backtesting Lies: Past performance really doesn't guarantee future results
  • Risk Management is Everything: The best strategy fails without proper position sizing
  • Markets Evolve: What worked last year might not work today
  • Emotions Still Matter: Even with bots, you'll feel the urge to intervene

The Technical Challenge

Building reliable trading infrastructure is fascinating. Real-time data feeds, execution latency, error handling when money is on the line - it's a unique engineering challenge that teaches you a lot about building robust systems.

November 2024

My Homelab Journey: From Chaos to Container

Every homelab starts the same way: "I just need a simple file server." Six months later, you're managing a dozen services, troubleshooting network issues at 2 AM, and loving every minute.

Why Self-Host?

In an age of cloud services, why run your own servers? For me, it comes down to learning, privacy, and the satisfaction of building something yourself.

Current Setup

  • Media: Jellyfin for streaming my media collection anywhere
  • Storage: Network-attached storage for backups and file sharing
  • Development: Local environments for testing projects
  • Monitoring: Keeping an eye on everything with dashboards

Docker Changed Everything

The move to containerization was a game-changer. Instead of conflicting dependencies and messy installations, everything runs isolated and reproducible. Updates are simple, backups are straightforward, and spinning up new services takes minutes.

The Learning Never Stops

That's the real value of a homelab. Every problem is an opportunity to learn something new about networking, security, or system administration. It's hands-on education that translates directly to real-world skills.
