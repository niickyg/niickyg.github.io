January 2025

Why I Started Mountain West Surface

Honestly, starting a business wasn't something I had planned out. It kind of just happened. I saw an opportunity and decided to go for it.

How It Started

Living in Park City, I noticed that a lot of people had concrete driveways and patios that were getting beat up by the weather. Utah winters are no joke. I figured there had to be a better way to help people protect their property, so I started learning everything I could about concrete sealing.

The Early Days

The first few months were all about figuring things out—learning the craft, getting my first clients, making mistakes and fixing them. It wasn't always smooth, but that's part of it. Every job taught me something new.

Where We're At Now

The business has grown a lot since those early days. We've got a solid reputation in Park City and I'm proud of the work we do. There's still a lot to learn and a lot of room to grow, and that's what keeps it exciting.

December 2024

Messing Around with Trading Bots

I got into automated trading as a side project. Not gonna lie, the idea of making money while you sleep sounded pretty good. Reality is a bit more complicated.

What I Learned

Building these systems taught me a lot—mostly about how unpredictable markets can be. Just because something worked in the past doesn't mean it'll work tomorrow.

  • Risk management matters more than strategy
  • Markets change, so your approach has to change too
  • It's harder than the YouTube gurus make it look

Still Worth It

Even if I'm not getting rich off it, the process of building and tweaking these systems is genuinely fun. It's a good way to stay sharp and learn about markets at the same time.

November 2024

My Homelab Setup

It started with wanting a simple media server. Now I've got a whole setup running at home. Classic scope creep, but no regrets.

What I'm Running

  • Media server for streaming movies and shows
  • Storage for backups and files
  • Random projects whenever I want to try something new

Why Bother?

Honestly, it's just fun. I like learning how things work, and running your own server teaches you a lot. Plus it's nice having everything under your own control instead of paying for a bunch of subscriptions.

Always a Work in Progress

That's the thing about homelabs—you're never really "done." There's always something to add or improve. It keeps things interesting.
